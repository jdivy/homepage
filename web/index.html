What brings home the bacon?

Jack is a programmer with many talents. He has a B.S. in Computer Science from Washington and Lee University and is currently pursuing a M.S. in Computer Science from Johns Hopkins University.

What languages does he speak?

Java is definitely his bread and butter at the moment, but Jack is very familiar with Groovy (makes sense) and dabbles in JavaScript either as part of Node.js or with the help of jQuery. He's also been known to throw together a website or two whether it be something simple (what you are looking at) or something with a little more meat like a Grails web application, so he has good command of html and css.

What tools does he use?

Jack currently uses git for SCM and IntelliJ IDEA for an IDE, but he has come from a long history of Eclipse and Subversion. His build tool of choice is Gradle, also a switch from a history of using Maven.

Anything else?

He is knowledgeable on many aspects of the software development lifecycle, with a particular focus on Agile practices. Somewhat related, he has much experience with set-up, maintenance, and use of automated build tools such as Hudson, Jenkins, and Atlassian Bamboo, as well as the entire Atlassian suite of development tools.

What would make Jack sell his kidney on the black market?

Assuming he knew how to access "the black market", quite a bit.

His wife needs a kidney.

Well, in this case he would give his kidney away for free and avoid the black market altogether. Needless to say, Jack loves his wife. Whether or not his kidney would fit is an entirely different question.

He could work for himself.

A bit of a pipe dream, Jack has always thought it would be nice to work for himself. While he has plenty of ideas, making the jump to a world without a regular paycheck is downright...well...scary.

Tonx coffee wants to expand.

Scenario. Business is booming and shipping all the way across the country from California is difficult. Opening a new location in Memphis, TN and hiring Jack to help run it is the perfect answer to a burgeoning roasting business.

Jack is a big fan of the Third Wave coffee movement. Although he doesn't give off the vibe, there is most certainly a little bit of "coffee snob" in him. He has been brewing single-origin coffees from many different roasters for a number of years. He was a Tonxian for a few iterations and loved every one! Sadly it became hard for him to justify the expense to his wife since he shared so much of it with his coworkers.

To this day he continues his routine of making Chemex coffee and distributing it to his teammates in demitasse cups.
